Research & Presentations

Asymptotic Symmetries in Loop Quantum Gravity

2023
Master's Project · Baylor University

Investigated supertranslations at spatial infinity in terms of Ashtekar–Barbero variables, connecting canonical loop quantum gravity to asymptotic symmetry structures more commonly studied in the string theory literature.

Investigations into the Relationship Between Loop Quantum Gravity and String Theory

2023
Presented · APS April Meeting

Explored points of contact between LQG's discrete geometric approach to quantum gravity and string theory's perturbative and non-perturbative frameworks.

Testing the Geometric Surface Conjecture for Rotating Traversable Wormholes

2022
Presented · APS April Meeting

Computational general relativity work parallelizing tensor calculations to evaluate wormhole and black hole invariants for rotating traversable wormhole geometries.

Black Hole Entropy of 2D Spin Networks

2022
Wolfram Community

Computational exploration of entropy in two-dimensional spin network models, connecting LQG's discrete quantum geometry to black hole thermodynamics.

Numerical CMB Contributions from Loop Quantum Cosmology

2021–2023
Baylor University

Numerically evaluated cosmic microwave background contributions from the loop quantum cosmology "big bounce" model as part of computational general relativity research at Baylor.

Even Self-Dual Lattices and Monstrous Moonshine

2023
Baylor Department of Mathematics, MTH 6367

Coursework investigation into the connections between even self-dual lattices, vertex operator algebras, and monstrous moonshine.

Uniform Asymptotic Approximation Method with Pöschl–Teller Potential

2023
Universe 9, no. 11

Applied uniform asymptotic approximation methods to scattering problems involving the Pöschl–Teller potential.

Casimir–Lifshitz Physics

2024–2025
Ensemble3 Center of Excellence, University of Warsaw

Computational quantum field theory work on the Casimir effect across different geometries, with applications to water phase transitions and molecular dynamics simulations — published in Physical Review A and presented at the Casimir Conference.
